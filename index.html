  • Randomized controlled and double-blinded study of Caphosol versus saline oral rinses in pediatric patients with cancer

Background: Oral mucositis (OM) is a significant side-effect of cancer treatment. The purpose of this study was to compare topically administered Caphosol to saline oral rinses in the prevention of mucositis in pediatric cancer patients undergoing chemotherapy.
Procedure: A controlled, double-blinded and randomized clinical study recruited patients between 2 to 17.99 years of age who were diagnosed with a malignancy. All patients were receiving either high-dose methotrexate, anthracycline or cisplatin chemotherapy (NCT0280733). All patients received two seven-day cycles of the mouth rinses, i.e. one cycle of Caphosol and one cycle of saline in a randomized order. Oral changes and symptoms were evaluated using the World Health Organisation (WHO) toxicity scale and the Children’s International Mucositis Evaluation Scale (ChIMES) scale. The primary endpoint was the frequency and severity of OM and oral symptoms.
Results: A total of 56 patients were recruited to the study, of whom 45 were randomized, with a median age of 6.5 years (range 2.1 to 17.1 years). No cases of severe OM were observed. Grade ≥3 oral symptoms were present at least once in 6 (13%) patients during the Caphosol cycle and 13 (29%) patients during the saline cycle (p=0.12). The peak of symptom scores was evident at around day 4 to 7 after administration of the chemotherapy with no marked differences between the rinse solutions. Multivariable regression analysis did not indicate a benefit of using Caphosol over the saline solution.
Conclusions: Caphosol rinse was not superior to saline in preventing OM or associated symptoms.

  • Octahedral distortion driven by CsPbI3 nanocrystal reaction temperature: The effects on phase stability and beyond

Cesium lead iodide (CsPbI3) perovskite nanocrystals (NCs) has demonstrated unique properties suitable for a variety of photovoltaic applications. However, its relatively low phase stability leads to a significant and fast drop in performance. Therefore, tackling the stability issue is an imperative step toward the development of more sustainable photovoltaic devices. Although, while many intricate approaches involving compositional and/or NC surface engineering have been proposed, the influence of the reaction temperature has been overlooked so far. By tuning the reaction temperature within the typically reported, for hot-injection synthesis, range (120–170 °C), we studied the phase stability and changes in the photovoltaic utilizing NCs in dispersions, films, and the solar cells. Importantly, we identified the optimum temperature (150°C) to boost the phase stability by up to a month under the ambient conditions. The improved stability was reflected in the optical properties of the dispersions, as well as in the unchanged performance of solar cells (up to 26 days of shelf storage in dry air). Findings are supported by the observed lowest octahedral distortions of NCs synthesized at 150 °C studied via structural refinement. This work demonstrates not only the approach to significantly increase the NCs stability, but also emphasizes the need for detailed studies of the synthesis parameters to enhance the performance of corresponding optoelectronic devices.

  • Cross-structural Factor-topic Model: Document Analysis with Sophisticated Covariates

Modern text data is increasingly gathered in situations where it is paired with a high-dimensional collection of covariates: then both the text, the covariates, and their relationships are of interest to analyze. Despite the growing amount of such data, current topic models are unable to take into account large amounts of covariates successfully: they fail to model structure among covariates and distort findings of both text and covariates. This paper presents a solution: a novel factor-topic model that enables researchers to analyze latent structure in both text and sophisticated document-level covariates collectively. The key innovation is that besides learning the underlying topical structure, the model also learns the underlying factorial structure from the covariates and the interactions between the two structures. A set of tailored variational inference algorithms for efficient computation are provided. Experiments on three different datasets show the model outperforms comparable topic models in the ability to predict held-out document content. Two case studies focusing on Finnish parliamentary election candidates and game players on Steam demonstrate the model discovers semantically meaningful topics, factors, and their interactions. The model both outperforms state-of-the-art models in predictive accuracy and offers new factor-topic insights beyond other topic models.

  • What, How and Why of Teacher leadership in the context of Higher Education: theoretical review

The current research aims to provide a theoretical review on the topic of teacher leadership in the context of higher education. Meta-synthesis technique is used to analyse research articles that introduced pieces of research dealing with teacher leadership at the higher education level. Using the model of golden circle of leadership that is rooted in the principles of authenticity in particular, the study creates a new conceptual model for teacher leadership. The model reflects complexity of the phenomenon of teacher leadership and suggests flexible approach to understanding of teacher leadership manifestations in teacher profession in higher education institutions. In addition, results of the theoretical review might inspire higher education organisations recognise and pay attention to teacher leaders, understand their constructive role in organisations and educational communities, value their input into educational practice and policy.
